1. Editing MIDI Notes and Velocities

One of my favourite aspects of MIDI editing is changing the velocities of the notes. This can help make your synths sound more interesting, or your drums sound more realistic.

You can also turn off the grid to get some crazy offbeat rhythms, or make instruments sound more lifelike by taking human imperfection into account.

2. Haas effect with Simple Delay

The Haas effect is a psychoacoustic phenomenon discovered by Dr. Helmut Haas in 1949. When one sound is followed by another by a delay time of approx. 40 ms or less, listeners perceive it as a single sound. It results in the stereo image of a sound being made wider.
